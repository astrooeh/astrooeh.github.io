selected publications

  1. A&A
    Improving the stellar age determination through joint modeling of binarity and asteroseismology: Grid modeling of the seismic red giant binary KIC 9163796
    D. H. Grossmann, P. G. Beck, S. Mathur, and 12 more authors
    Astronomy & Astrophysics, Apr 2025
    Abs DOI arXiv Bib

    Ages of single giants derived from isochrone fitting typically carry uncertainties of 30 to 50 percent. By combining the constraints from binarity, spectroscopy and asteroseismology in a multidimensional grid of stellar models, we determine an age of 2.44 Gyr for the red giant and subgiant binary KIC 9163796, corresponding to a relative age error of 9 percent. Both components share an initial helium abundance well above the primordial value, and the model masses agree with those from the asteroseismic scaling relations.

  2. A&A
    Ensemble Individual Mode Frequency Modeling of Kepler Giants
    D. H. Grossmann, S. Mathur, P. G. Beck, and 7 more authors
    Astronomy & Astrophysicsunder review , Jun 2026
    Abs Bib

    Global seismic modeling of red giants leaves random age uncertainties of roughly 20 to 50 percent, and the systematics are rarely quantified. We extend seismic modeling to individual mode frequencies for red giant branch stars, validate the method against literature targets, and apply it to around 100 Kepler red giants of approximately solar mass and metallicity. We obtain average random uncertainties of 3.4 percent in mass, 1.3 percent in radius and 13.2 percent in age, and we find that ages from individual mode frequency modeling are on average 14 percent larger than those from global seismic modeling. Modeling individual mode frequencies improves age precision by a factor of two, providing stronger constraints for galactic archaeology.
